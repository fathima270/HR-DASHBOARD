overview: A data-driven HR Analytics dashboard created in Power BI to track workforce demographics, attrition rates, and compensation across different departments.

Core Metrics (KPIs): Features high-level summary cards showing 8,375 total working years, 2,851 work-life balance points, and a total monthly income of $4M.

Attrition Analysis: A bar chart breaks down employee turnover by age, highlighting that the 26-35 age group experiences the highest attrition, while the 55+ group is the most stable.

Educational Background: An area chart displays the distribution of staff expertise, showing a heavy concentration in Life Sciences and Medical fields compared to Technical or HR degrees.

Departmental Insights: A column chart compares job levels across sectors, revealing that Research & Development is the largest department, followed by Sales and Human Resources.

Salary & Role Mapping: A frequency plot visualizes the count of job roles against Monthly Rates, helping to identify pay equity and salary distribution patterns across the company.

Interactive Tools: Includes functional Slicers (filters) for selecting specific departments and a Range Slider for filtering data by monthly income levels.

Operational Ratios: A pie chart illustrates the relationship between Daily Rates and Standard Hours, providing a visual representation of labor costs.

Development Tools: Built using Power BI, utilizing DAX for custom calculations and Power Query for data transformation and cleaning.
